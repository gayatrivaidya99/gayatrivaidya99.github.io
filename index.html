Gayatri Vaidya

PhD Researcher · George Mason University · Humanitarian Informatics Lab · Fairfax, VA

Gayatri Vaidya is a PhD researcher in Information Technology at George Mason University, advised by Dr. Hemant Purohit. My research focuses on how AI systems can support emergency management — building tools that help first responders and affected communities navigate crises through structured knowledge and natural language interfaces.

I develop Evac-AI, a conversational AI system for flood evacuation intelligence in Virginia Beach, combining a Neo4j knowledge graph, local LLM inference (Ollama, llama3.1:8b), and a SetFit fine-tuned intent classifier to answer natural language queries about flood risk zones, evacuation routes, and emergency shelters — grounded in real government data from FEMA, NOAA, VDEM, and Virginia Beach GIS.

A parallel project transforms the Virginia Beach Emergency Operations Plan into a structured inter-agency coordination knowledge graph using spaCy-based NER and LLM-based triple extraction.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Packaged App Development Analyst at Accenture, building scalable ETL/ELT pipelines. I hold an MS in Computer Science from GMU (2025).
